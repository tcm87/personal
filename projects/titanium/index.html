Bicycle Frame Table

Description

A number of years ago, I bought two broken bicycles off craigslist for $100. Over the years, I found a use for the parts from both bicycles, except for the severely cracked frame of the titanium road bicycle that sat untouched in the shed. I thought about posting the frame back on craigslist for someone to use as an art piece. Then I thought, “I have the whole summer of 2016 ahead of me, why don’t I turn it into something cool?”

While I have experience with furniture design and fabrication, I had never worked with titanium as a furniture material and at this scale. I find its material properties fascinating (and frustrating when it comes to machining): the 1/2in glass table top weighs more than 2.5 times as much as the entire titanium table frame. No wonder titanium is renowned as a frame material for bicycles.

When it comes to TIG welding titanium alloy, the learning curve is steep. It takes incredible control and proper conditions to ensure a proper, strong weld. I only had a handful of hours to practice before welding the real thing, so I tacked the frame pieces together instead of welding the entire seam. While these initial welds are adequately strong, I look forward to laying down full beads as I improve my welding skills in the future.

Info

Built using lathe, manual milling machine, TIG welder, waterjet
Reused titanium bicycle frame and 3/2.5 alloy tube, 1/2in thick glass | 21in x 16in x 15in

Design Process

While I've wanted to do something with the broken bicycle frame for a while, the actual construction took place in the final few weeks of my 2016 summer break.
