Custom Furniture.

Titanium. Steel. Wood. My favorite medium.

I love everything about furniture- the materiality, the scale, and the ergonomic component that is required by good design.

Below are a few of my favorites.

Years ago I bought a broken bicycle off craigslist. I used all the parts except the cracked titanium frame, which sat collecting dust for the next few years. In the summer of 2016, I decided to make something of the frame.

While I have experience with furniture design and fabrication, I had never worked with titanium as a furniture material and at this scale. I find its material properties fascinating (and frustrating when it comes to machining): the 1/2in glass table top weighs more than 2.5 times as much as the entire titanium table frame. No wonder titanium is renowned as a frame material for bicycles.

When it comes to TIG welding titanium alloy, it takes incredible control and proper conditions to ensure a proper weld. I only had a handful of hours to practice before welding the real table, so I tacked the frame pieces together instead of welding the entire bead. While these initial welds are adequately strong, I look forward to laying down full beads as I improve my welding skill in the future.

The lasercut base dictates the type of objects placed above. The table legs create compound angles as they span outward from the center of the table. I used a milling machine and TIG welder to achieve the exact angles and precise welds necessary for the stability of the table.

Central ascension from the intersection of mirrored angles forms the basketweave pattern.
