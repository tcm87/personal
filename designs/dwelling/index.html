DESIGN ABOUT

An island refuge for six.

The final project of Arch 1101 Design I Studio allowed me to tie together all my previous work during the semester, and push the concept of touch to the next level.

The Derivative

Following my research into sensory substitution, and the actual drawing generated by my sensory augmentation device, the topography of the environment becomes a series of irregular marks within equally spaced channels. The density, continuity, and weight of the ink changes, but they always reside within the same channel associated with their appendage.

I took these ideas of embedded structure, of channels that change in density and spatial quality, and applied it to my architecture.

Fetish Models

Studies into materiality and form. Each model is a distinct interpretation of the drawing generated by my sensory augmentation device.

The Program

The architectural program and design revolves around a hypothetical island home for six distinct friends, each with an occupation that specializes in one of the five senses. This island house will be used from time to time to thoughtfully explore their apprehension of the world.

The architecture of the island is to be comprised of a series of spaces, constructions, and environments intended to appeal to a complex network of the senses, inevitably more than one at a time.

The Design

The main structure is defined by extension in one direction, aligned with the spine of my island. While the structure resides in a single plane, it steps in and out of the island topography.

The secondary element of brass orthogonal planes act as false topographic manipulations that carve into both site and structure, defining new spaces and restricting other areas.
