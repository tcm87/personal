ABOUT

Bio

My first memorable foray into design came in the fifth grade, when I would draw crazy combat robot designs on the margins of binder paper. I waited two years until I was able to turn those drawings into reality by cutting metal and soldering electronics in the garage with my father. From there, my designs ricocheted off the walls of my environment. However, I am more than just a designer. I was a competitive distance runner throughout high school, and I am an avid cyclist, from fixed gear to cyclocross. And of course, I love my San Francisco coffee shops– there is no better way to start a morning than with a latte and slice of country toast.

Design Philosophy

My diverse body of work has given me valuable insight into the essential question of what is good design. Early in high school, I learned about the Bauhaus movement and its principle “form follows function.” Over the years, as I have both designed and created, my personal design philosophy has strayed from such bare-bones simplicity. I believe that form and function can coexist in symbiotic ways. Beauty can inspire and complement, as long as the function has definite purpose.
