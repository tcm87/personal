Experience Design at SF MOMA

December 18, 2017

On the seventh floor of the SF MOMA, eight musicians begin to play, each recorded in a separate room of a classical mansion. The separate tracks of each musician are synced in a single continuous session. Eight screens throughout the exhibit showcase the solo musicians, with individual speakers playing their separate tracks. As you walk around the exhibit, different intruments and voices are brought into focus, while others blend into the background.

This exhibit made me pause, convince me to wander through space, and close my eyes to the ebb and flow of the stripped down music. The blend of the senses, and the communal experience was one-of-a-kind, nothing I have experienced in a museum before. I hope to capture the same emotions in my own designs.

June 2017

I’ve worked with wood, steel, titanium even. I’m very familiar with the processes involved to shape and combine these materials. However, I’ve never worked with fiberglass before.
